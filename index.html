What makes blockchain special?

Blockchains are beneficial because they are:

Decentralized

Secure

Scalable

Decentralized

A decentralized system is a system without a middleman. So all transactions, regulations and communication is peer-to-peer directly (pals only club!)

Do you use Spotify? Then you’re probably aware of how poorly Spotify artists are paid. This, my dear, is because Spotify is a major middleman (almost a monopolist) between a user and an artist, charging large fees for artists to host their content. So basically when you pay for your subscription on Spotify, only a few cents are going to the artists you listen to. But with NFTs it is completely different. Because there is no such major middleman, you can pay the artist easily and without any extra charges. Just as if you were paying them cash in real world.

Secure

A secure system is one that can’t be attacked or can fend it off

Want to buy a diamond? But also want to know exactly where it came from? Have no fear, for blockchain is here. Blockchain can track and authenticate diamonds, gems, and other metals through the global supply chain. Before, ring owners relied on certificates to prove authencity or that a diamond is sourced from a conflict-free zone. Soon, ring owners will be able to look up their rings on the same blockchain. You’ll be able to track exactly where your diamond has been with a permanent and unchangable record.

Scalable

A scalable system is one that can grow, handle a lot of operations and do it fast (a growing boi)

Have you ever tried to get a loan from the bank? Then you know how much paperwork it requires, how long the process is and how silly mistakes can overcomplicate the process or result in rejection. Blockchain allows you to do it more efficiently because all the necessary information is automatically recorded and stored. This eliminates silly mistakes, reduces costs and allows to handle more operations.

Consensus 1

Proof-of-work

POW

Cryptographic hash algorithm based problem solvers (Miners) compete to discover the hash of the next block.

The one with the higher computer power is more likely to find the hash quicker and get the reward.

Consensus 2

Proof-of-stake

POS

System arbitrary selects a node as a validator for the next block.

The one with the biggest stake is more likely to be selected and get the reward.
